Apollo 11 Information

Welcome to a page that brings you some information about Apollo 11, as well as gives some pictures and a 3d model of some artifacts used in the mission.

Introduction

The Apollo 11 mission marked a pivotal moment in human history, yielding remarkable scientific discoveries and groundbreaking technological advancements.

Command Center

The command module, named Columbia, was the only part of the Apollo 11 spacecraft to return to Earth. It served as the living quarters for the three astronauts — Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins — for most of the mission. While Armstrong and Aldrin descended to the lunar surface in the Lunar Module Eagle, Collins remained in the Columbia, orbiting the Moon and maintaining communication with both the crew on the surface and Mission Control on Earth.

Gloves

The A7-L spacesuit worn by Neil Armstrong during the Apollo 11 mission had two types of gloves: intravehicular (IV) gloves for use inside the spacecraft and extravehicular (EV) gloves for use on the lunar surface. The EV gloves were critically important for enabling Armstrong to perform tasks on the Moon and were designed with specific features to protect him from the harsh lunar environment.

Suit

The Neil Armstrong spacesuit, designated A7-L, was a marvel of engineering designed to provide a life-sustaining environment for the astronaut during lunar exploration. It consisted of multiple layers and components carefully integrated to protect Armstrong from the harsh conditions of the lunar surface.
